The Chancellor’s **Autumn Budget 2025** introduces major tax and policy reforms affecting individuals, landlords, and businesses across the UK.

## Tax and Savings Changes in the Autumn Budget 2025

### Income Tax and National Insurance

- Income Tax and National Insurance thresholds frozen until **2031**
- More people likely to move into higher tax bands due to fiscal drag
- Increased long-term tax exposure for working professionals and business owners

### ISA Allowance Reductions (From April 2027)

- Under-65s limited to **£12,000** tax-free
- Over-65s retain **£20,000**
- Reduced flexibility for tax-efficient savings

Local advice is increasingly important when planning long-term savings.

### Pension Salary Sacrifice Changes (From April 2029)

- NI relief capped at **£2,000 per month**
- Reduced benefits for higher earners
- Greater need for professional pension and tax planning

---

## Property and Lifestyle Tax Changes in Scotland

### Mansion Tax on High-Value Properties

- Homes over **£2 million** face annual charges of **£2,500–£7,500** from 2028
- Impacts landlords, property investors, and high-value homeowners

### Electric Vehicle Mileage Tax

- New EV mileage tax replacing fuel duty
- Affects company vehicles and business fleets
- Important for contractors and SMEs using electric vehicles

## Business and Employer Impacts in Paisley and Glasgow

### National Living Wage Increase

- Rising to **£12.71 per hour** from April 2026
- Higher payroll and pension obligations
- Increased pressure on small business cash flow

### Sector-Specific Tax Increases

- Remote gaming duty: **40%**
- Online betting tax: **25%**
- Higher compliance and reporting costs

Local businesses should review staffing budgets and pricing strategies early.
